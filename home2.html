Why ICT Education at Namilyango Needs a Rethink

Most students finish school without ever writing a line of code. We investigated why — and built a solution.

Limited Learning resources
Namilyango cCollege lacks enough computers in the IT lab , making hands-on code learning nearly impossible.
58%
of surveyed students did not know how to code
Outdated Curriculum
ICT syllabi focus on theory over practice. Students memorise definitions but never build anything real.
4 in 5
students couldn't name a single programming language
Teacher Training Gap
ICT teachers often teach from textbooks with limited practical experience, limiting what students can learn.
65%
of ICT teachers have not done a coding course themselves
The Mobile Opportunity
While computers are scarce, smartphone ownership is rising fast — creating a new pathway for digital learning.
89%
of students have access to a smartphone or shared device

How CodeLearn Works

A three-step journey from total beginner to confident coder — designed to work on any device.

01
Pick a Learning Path
Students choose from beginner, intermediate, or advanced ICT tracks. Each path adapts to their current knowledge level.
02
Learn Through Doing
Interactive mini-lessons, coding challenges, and real-world scenarios replace passive reading with active problem-solving.
03
Track & Earn
Badges, progress bars, and leaderboards keep students motivated. Teachers can monitor class performance in real time.

How We Researched This Problem

This project is grounded in real data collected from students and teachers at Namilyango College.

  • Surveys & Interviews
    We surveyed 120 students and 15 ICT teachers at Namilyango College to understand current challenges and learning habits.
  • Literature Review
    We reviewed the national ICT curriculum guidelines, NITA-U reports, and global research on gamified learning outcomes.
  • Prototype Testing
    An early version of CodeLearn was tested with 30 students over two weeks. We measured engagement and quiz performance.
  • Data Analysis
    Results were compared before and after using the platform. Test scores improved by an average of 34% among regular users.

Built for Real Classrooms

Every feature was designed based on what students and teachers said they actually need.

Gamified Learning
Points, badges, and streaks make learning addictive. Students compete on leaderboards and unlock new content as they progress.
Mobile-First Design
Works on any smartphone browser. No app download needed. Students can learn from home even without a computer.
Interactive Quizzes
Drag-and-drop coding exercises and instant feedback help students learn from mistakes in real time.
Teacher Dashboard
Teachers see each student's progress, time spent, and weak areas — making it easy to give targeted support.
Offline Mode
Core lessons are cached so students can keep learning even when internet connectivity drops.
Certifications
Students earn shareable digital certificates when they complete each module — building a real ICT portfolio.
